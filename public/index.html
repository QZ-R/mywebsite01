Biography

Qing-Zeng Zhu is a Postdoc of organic geochemistry at the Hinrichslab. His research interests include stable isotope probing (SIP), organic matter (OM) fate in marine sediments, and geomicrobiology. He is currently working on a project about secondary production and its role in reshaping the OM pool after the deposition from primary production in the ocean.

Secondary Production in Marine Sediments

Secondary production in sediments may have been underestimated before. This process dominated by bacterial activities plays an pivotal role in reshaping the organic matter composition after sedimentation.

The Ocean Floor - Earth’s Uncharted Interface

This project is under the clusters-of-excellence and it aims to embark on a new chapter in ocean-floor research by “quantifying exchange processes at this significant boundary layer and their roles in the Earth system”

DARCLIFE

DARCLIFE (Deep subsurface Archaea: carbon cycle, life strategies, and role in sedimentary ecosystems) aims to study benthic archaea, their carbon cycle and life strategies in the deep subseafloor. It is a fruitful project which involves an interdisciplinary team of scientists from the fields of biogeochemistry, earth sciences, and microbiology.
